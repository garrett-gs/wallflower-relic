Our Story
More than rentals
Garrett had been building custom pieces for event planners when a recurring problem became impossible to ignore — clients were paying fabrication prices for something they'd never use again. He knew a rental business made sense, but had no idea where to start.

Crystal was running her business the way most creatives do — doing everything herself, never enough hours in the day. People kept telling her she should meet Garrett. Those same people kept telling Garrett he should reach out to Crystal.

It was Crystal who finally made the call. Garrett followed up with coffee. That meeting lasted nearly four hours — and what started as a conversation about filling gaps turned into a realization that they had built exactly what the other was missing.

Wallflower Relic was the natural result.

Some build frames, others create the moments that go into them. What happens when they meet?
the builder

R E L I C brings the foundation: thoughtfully engineered custom fabrications, built with precision, durability, and longevity in mind. Every piece begins with structure — measured, tested, and made to last.

the creator

Wallflower brings the vision: a curated collection of elevated rentals designed to shape a space, set a tone, and turn moments into experiences. Each piece is selected and styled with an eye for story, balance, and impact.

Together, we create high-end rentals and custom builds that don't just fill a room — they define it.

This collaboration was born from a shared respect for the process: fewer pieces, better made. Less excess, more intention. Quality over quantity, always. Our work lives at the intersection of form and function — where beauty is supported by strength, and design is grounded in craftsmanship.

We believe the best events are built the same way as the best businesses:

with care, clarity, and people you trust.

Welcome to what happens when the frame and the moment meet.

What Goes Into Our Work

Before we talk numbers, we want you to understand what's actually involved. Every installation is different — here's a look at what goes into the work we do.

Before the numbers
Let's talk about scope.

One of the biggest reasons event pricing can feel confusing is because scope changes everything.

Two installations may look similar online — but the size, materials, labor hours, and design complexity behind them can be completely different. That's why pricing in this industry varies so widely.

Statement installations are rarely a single line item.

Most proposals include several components:

Design & renderings
Materials & sourcing
Fabrication labor
Delivery & transport
Installation & strike services

Each of these elements contributes to the final investment.

Statement Frames
The 8ft × 8ft Frame

One of the most popular installation sizes. Commonly used for:

Statement seating charts
Shelved display walls
Escort card displays
Back of bars
Typical Investment Range
$2,750 – $5,000+

Final investment depends on materials, design complexity, and installation scope.

What can affect this price:
Custom wallpaper or printed panels
Acrylic name displays or layered materials
Dimensional architectural panels
Custom décor or shelving elements
Integrated lighting features
Textile installation add-ons
Large guest counts requiring more display pieces

Scope changes everything — two frames of the same size can be very different projects.

Statement Frames
The 8ft × 12ft Frame

A larger-scale installation designed to anchor the room and create a strong visual focal point. Commonly used for:

Statement seating charts
Shelved display walls
Escort card & wine window displays
Back of bars
Typical Investment Range
$4,750 – $8,500+

Same scope variables apply — materials, complexity, and installation needs drive the final number.

Scope additions that affect pricing:
Custom wallpaper or printed panels
Acrylic name displays or layered materials
Dimensional architectural panels
Custom décor or shelving elements
Integrated lighting features
Textile installation add-ons
Large guest counts requiring more display pieces

The step up in size significantly increases material quantities and labor time.

Custom Builds
Bar Installations

Our bars are built using a modular system — combining architectural components to create the size, layout, and design that fits your event.

From intimate bar moments to large-scale environments, each installation can be tailored through custom facades, finishes, lighting, shelving, and decorative elements.

Bar pricing varies widely — fixed rental bars often fall below these ranges, while fully custom builds can exceed them.

Typical investment ranges:
Standard Bar & Back of Bar (under 10ft)
$4,000 – $8,000+
Large / Mega Bar (16–32ft)
$6,000 – $15,000+
Pricing varies based on facade design, modular size, shelving, specialty finishes, integrated lighting, and acrylic elements.
Scale
Large-Scale Installations (12ft+)

Once installations reach a certain scale, pricing becomes far more dependent on the unique scope of the project.

At this size, installations often require additional structural design, increased material quantities, and significantly more fabrication and installation labor.

Because of this, large-scale installations are custom quoted — not priced within a standard range. Every project is approached as its own design challenge.

What influences these quotes:
Structural engineering and fabrication complexity
Material quantities and specialty finishes
Installation crew size and labor hours
Venue access and logistics
Custom design development

Every large-scale installation is approached as a custom design project — which is why pricing is evaluated individually.

Fabric & Draping
Textile Art & Fabric Installations

Textile art installations vary widely depending on scale, materials, and complexity. Small accent moments and large immersive environments require very different amounts of fabric, preparation, and labor.

Unlike traditional draping, textile art installations involve intentionally sculpting and designing fabric within the space to create a more integrated design moment.

Typical investment ranges:
On Existing Structures
Existing structures, architectural features, table moments
$950 – $5,000+
per location
With Custom Structures
Custom frames, large backdrops, immersive environments
$4,000 – $10,000+
per location

Scale matters — a small sweetheart table moment is a very different project from a full reception textile environment.
